Safety Precautions during a Natural Disaster

General Preparedness

  • Emergency Kit: Prepare an emergency kit with essentials like water, non-perishable food, medications, flashlight, batteries, first aid supplies, important documents, and cash.
  • Communication Plan: Establish a family communication plan, including how to get in touch with each other and where to meet if separated.
  • Stay Informed: Keep up with weather alerts and warnings through radio, TV, or a reliable mobile app.

Earthquakes

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On: During shaking, drop to your hands and knees, cover your head and neck with your arms, and take shelter under a sturdy table or against an interior wall. Hold on until the shaking stops.
  • Stay Indoors: If you're indoors, stay there. If outdoors, move to an open area away from buildings, streetlights, and utility wires.
  • After the Shaking Stops: Be prepared for aftershocks. Check yourself and others for injuries and provide first aid if needed. Evacuate the building if it’s unsafe.

Floods

  • Move to Higher Ground: If you’re in a flood-prone area, move to higher ground immediately.
  • Avoid Floodwaters: Don’t walk, swim, or drive through floodwaters. Just six inches of moving water can knock you down, and one foot of moving water can sweep your vehicle away.
  • Turn Off Utilities: If time permits, turn off utilities at the main switches or valves.

Hurricanes

Evacuate if Advised: Follow evacuation orders from local authorities.
Secure Your Home: Close storm shutters, and secure outdoor objects that could become projectiles. Consider moving to a safe room if your home is not secure.
Stay Indoors: During the storm, stay inside and away from windows and doors.

Tornadoes

Seek Shelter: Go to a small, windowless interior room on the lowest level of your home, like a basement or storm cellar. If you're in a mobile home, evacuate to a sturdier building.
Cover Your Head: Protect yourself from flying debris by covering your head and neck with your arms.
Stay Low: Crouch down and stay low to the ground.

Wildfires

Evacuate Early: If there's a wildfire nearby, evacuate as soon as possible. Don't wait for official orders if you feel unsafe.
Protect Your Home: Remove flammable materials from your yard, close all doors and windows, and turn off gas supplies if possible.
Wear Protective Clothing: If you're caught outside, wear protective clothing to shield yourself from heat and embers.

Extreme Heat

Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water, even if you’re not thirsty.
Stay Indoors: Stay in an air-conditioned building as much as possible. If you don’t have AC, visit a cooling center or public building.
Limit Outdoor Activities: Avoid strenuous activities during the hottest part of the day.

Winter Storms

Stay Warm: Dress in layers and stay indoors as much as possible. If you must go outside, wear appropriate clothing to avoid frostbite and hypothermia.
Drive Safely: If you must travel, do so during the day, keep others informed of your route, and carry an emergency kit in your vehicle.
Prevent Carbon Monoxide Poisoning: Never use a generator, grill, or camp stove indoors.

Landslides

Evacuate Immediately: If you suspect a landslide, evacuate immediately to higher ground.
Stay Alert: Listen to local warnings and stay informed about the risks in your area.
Be Cautious After a Slide: Avoid the area of the landslide, as it may still be unstable.

Volcanic Eruptions

Evacuate if Advised: Follow local evacuation orders.
Protect Yourself from Ash: Wear long-sleeved shirts, pants, and a mask or damp cloth to avoid inhaling ash. Stay indoors if possible.
Stay Away from the Volcano: Avoid areas downwind and downstream of the volcano.

Tsunamis

Move to Higher Ground: If you feel an earthquake near a coast or see a sudden receding of water, move to higher ground immediately.
Follow Evacuation Orders: If a tsunami warning is issued, evacuate coastal areas.
Stay Away from the Shore: Do not return to shore until authorities declare it safe.

Additional Tips:

Know Your Local Risks: Understand the specific natural disasters that could impact your area and prepare accordingly.
Practice Drills: Regularly practice what to do in case of a disaster with your family.
Stay Calm: In any disaster, staying calm and thinking clearly can make a significant difference in your safety.
